Project Background

Overview

Exploring new places, whether far or nearby, is something many of us love. But before the fun starts, there's always planning. According to the World Tourism Organization (UNWTO), there were 1.5 billion international tourists in 2021, while domestic tourism accounted for a whopping 8 billion trips. However, the process from planning a trip to embarking on it is often filled with stress and anxiety.

A 2022 survey by Skift revealed that a significant 70% of respondents found holiday planning stressful. With such a significant portion of people feeling overwhelmed by the searching & planning process, there's an evident need for a streamlined solution.

Challenge

In today's fast-paced world, there's a sea of information available at our fingertips. With countless places to visit, restaurants/cafes to try, and events/shows to join, making a choice becomes a task in itself. This vast array of options can lead to confusion and the fear of missing out (FOMO) on great experiences. 60% of people struggle to find authentic real time experiences because, despite being in the city, users often miss out on local events or attractions, emphasizing a disconnect between real-time happenings and traveler awareness. The recommendations they receive are often disjointed, outdated, or not tailored to their preferences.

Additionally, the itinerary planning process itself has become overly complicated, leading to frustration and a lack of real-time insights. Explorers or travelers are inundated with excessive information, yet they often find this information isn't personalized to their needs. Furthermore, when disruptions occur during their trips, current platform lack in alternate plan suggestions, so they feel unprepared and unsupported.

Travelers often struggle to truly connect with the essence, culture, and community of a city. These factors collectively lead to missed chances, unnecessary squandering of time, and increased stress levels even before one begins their journey to explore new destinations.

Problem Statement

There's a notable gap in the market where users lack latest information, face difficulties in itinerary planning, and have no alternate plan when disruptions occur. In short, People need a way to effortlessly discover authentic travel experiences, but current solutions bury them in clutter and clichÃ©s.

"How might we streamline real-time discovery and planning for local explorers and any travelers so that they can effortlessly uncover both renowned and hidden gems, from attractions and events to eateries, in their vicinity?"

Vision

My goal is to transforming every journey into a spontaneous adventure, connects explorers or travelers to the heartbeat of any city in the world, ensuring they immerse themselves in authentic experiences beyond the traditional guidebook.

Outcome

ExploreEase - An intuitive, engaging travel app that simplifies discovery and planning, enhancing every journey with personalized, real-time insights and seamless community connection.

Design Thinking Process

User Research

Market Analysis

Embarking on my journey to redefine the travel experiences, I first immersed myself in extensive desk research to grasp the current market landscape. On the Desk Research front, I've undertaken extensive Market Research, familiarizing myself with the evolving dynamics of the travel sector. Furthermore, I've identified and analyzed key competitors, understanding their core offerings and market positioning.

My market analysis was an eye-opener: By 2023, the global tourism sector will hit $2.3 trillion, with the U.S. contributing $190.4 billion. Meanwhile, Asia Pacific's demand is predicted to lead through 2031.

Driving this are trends: a focus on local travel, a rise in AI-empowered travel platforms, and a $855 billion post-COVID travel boom.

Competitor Analysis

My Competitor Analysis wasn't just a superficial overview; I delved deep, leveraging a SWOT Analysis to evaluate competitors holistically, from their strengths and weaknesses to potential opportunities and threats.

I found that current travel platforms exhibit key gaps: such as, inflexibility in dynamic planning, an overload of non-personalized information, and outdated interfaces. Many lack real-time updates, leaving travelers unprepared for unexpected changes. Despite numerous apps, there's a clear demand for a unified platform that blends discovery with planning, offering authentic, real-time recommendations in a user-friendly format.

Dominant players in the digital travel planning space included TripAdvisor, TripIt, Yelp, and Expedia, while Google Maps remained crucial for real-time exploration assistance. TripAdvisor reported a remarkable 224 million visits in a single month, with around 80% of travelers spending nearly four weeks researching destinations.

Despite a crowded market, there's a strong need for a unified, comprehensive, user-friendly platform that seamlessly integrates discovery with day planning.

Primary Research has been instrumental in gaining firsthand insights. This feedback loop offered a window into the desired features and functionalities that travelers and explorers seek. These insights were fundamental in shaping the app's features and functionality, ensuring they align closely with the real needs and preferences of users.

Concept

My target users are diverse, ranging from tech-savvy millennials to seasoned travelers, essentially anyone aged 20-70 with a smartphone, seeking an enhanced, stress-free travel and exploration experience.

Visual Designs

Design Principles

The visual design of ExploreEase adheres to key principles: Accessibility, Ease of use, Understanding, Simplicity, and Consistency throughout. To make sure the app is both useful and aesthetically pleasing, every component is designed to complement these ideals.


Style Guides

In crafting the style guide, I meticulously selected a color palette and design elements that align with the app's vision of being user-friendly, inviting, and easy to navigate. Colors are blend of shades of greens, blues, and yellow & orangeI.

In addition, I have used pastel variations of the main color scheme to provide a more natural and soothing effect. This was a carefully considered choice meant to inspire a sense of peace and a closeness to nature, echoing the spirit of exploration and travel.

The main font, "Nunito" (with different typefaces) offers a clean and modern aesthetic, enhancing readability and user friendliness. This thoughtful pairing is designed to balance functionality with aesthetic appeal, contributing to the app's approachable and professional look.

Mid Fidelity Wireframes & Prototype

The creation of mid-fidelity wireframes was a critical step, focusing on refining user interface layouts and flows for optimal intuitiveness and usability. User Testing

Mid-Fidelity Prototype Testing

I conducted 8 user testing sessions with real users, an essential step in the development process. These sessions were designed to evaluate how actual users interacted with the app, providing invaluable insights into user behavior, preferences, and potential areas for improvement. In these testing, users engaged with the app, carrying out various tasks and providing feedback while I observed and documented their experiences.

Based on the responses and critiques received, I implemented essential changes to the design, aligning it more closely with user needs and expectations.

This process of integrating user feedback was vital in advancing the prototype from a mid-fidelity stage to a high-fidelity, fully functional version, significantly enhancing its usability and overall user experience.
